Frequently Asked Questions

We know you might have questions — here are some answers that may help you feel confident about taking the first step.

Do I need a referral from a doctor to come to your centre?

Not at all! You can contact us directly. We’ll schedule a consultation to understand your child’s needs and guide you on the best next steps.

What ages of children do you support?

We typically work with children ages 2–17, but every child is unique. If your child is outside this range, we’ll still happily meet with you to see how we can help or refer you to the right support.

Does your center provide both educational support and therapy services together?

Yes! We believe learning and therapy go hand in hand. Our integrated approach combines tailored educational guidance with therapeutic support, so your child can thrive academically, socially and emotionally - all under one welcoming roof.

What happens during the first visit?

The first session is all about listening — to you and your child. We’ll talk about your concerns, answer questions, and do a gentle observation to understand your child’s strengths and challenges.

How long are therapy sessions, and how often will my child attend?

Most sessions are 40-50 minutes. The frequency depends on your child’s needs and goals — it could be once a week or several times a week. We’ll work with your schedule to make it manageable and affordable.

How does Future Inclusive Center ensures the entire support is affordable for families?

We know every family’s situation is unique and we’re committed to making quality care accessible. Our fees are carefully designed to be affordable for families while ensuring your child receives expert, compassionate care every step of the way.

How will I know if my child is making progress?

We use a mix of observation, documented goals, and regular progress meetings with you. We also celebrate every win — no matter how small — because progress is more than numbers.

Do you offer support for siblings or the whole family?

Yes! We provide sibling workshops, family counselling sessions, and resources to help create a supportive home environment for everyone.

What if my child refuses to participate?

That’s okay — it happens sometimes. Our therapists are skilled at building trust and making sessions fun, so children often warm up quickly once they feel safe.

What makes Future Inclusive Centre different from other therapy centres?

We combine clinical expertise with genuine emotional connection. Therapy here isn’t just about skills — it’s about confidence, joy, and belonging. We’re a family, not just a service.
